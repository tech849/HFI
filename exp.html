Here's a comprehensive history of the Konark Sun Temple:


*Ancient History (1278 AD)*


- King Narasimhadeva I of the Eastern Ganga dynasty commissioned the temple's construction. - Built as a tribute to the Sun God, Surya. - Originally called the "Black Pagoda" due to its dark stone exterior.
*Architecture and Design*


- Designed as a giant chariot, symbolizing the Sun God's vehicle. - 24 wheels, each 10 feet in diameter, representing the 24 hours of the day. - Seven horses pulling the chariot, representing the seven days of the week and the seven colors of the rainbow. - Intricate carvings and sculptures depicting various aspects of life, mythology, and astronomy.
*Construction and Completion (1278-1290 AD)*


- Took 12 years to build. - 1,200 workers labored to construct the temple. - Original height: 228 feet (now 100 feet).
*Decline and Damage (14th-19th centuries)*



- Ravaged by Muslim invasions and natural disasters. - British colonial neglect and abandonment.
*Restoration and Preservation (19th-20th centuries)*


- Restoration efforts began in the 19th century. - Declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1984. - Ongoing conservation efforts by the Archaeological Survey of India.
*Cultural Significance*


- Represents Odisha's rich cultural heritage. - Symbolizes India's architectural excellence. - Embodies Hindu mythology and cosmology. - Showcases astronomical knowledge and precision.
*Key Features*

- Majestic entrance.




- Nata Mandir (dancing hall).





- Jagamohana (assembly hall).


- Deul (sanctum sanctorum).
